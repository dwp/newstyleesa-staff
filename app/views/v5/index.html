What we learned

Concern regarding the wording ‘cancelling’ of application. Any claim submitted must be decided upon unless withdrawn.

If a claim form is incomplete, legislation allows this to be corrected by the decision maker if they are able to contact the claimant by an appropriate means – phone, email etc otherwise it would be disallowed as defective.

If the claimant fails to sign the claimant commitment or fails to participate in an interview then the claim would be disallowed for the appropriate reason as per the legislation.

In relation to duplicate claims, a claim ceases to exist once it has been decided by the decision maker. If a claim is disallowed the decision disallowing it cannot be superseded because of a change in circumstances which occurred after the decision - a further claim may be made as a result of the change of circumstances. If a claim is allowed and a further claim is received the decision maker should consider whether or not to admit it in order to establish whether an application is hopeless and if admitted the decision maker should either make a decision to supersede where the outcome is changed or make a decision not to supersede where the outcome is not changed. NB If the application is not admitted because it is hopeless, the decision maker should notify the claimant that no decision has been made and there is no right of appeal.
