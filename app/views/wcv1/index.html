Work coach view – version 1

What we want to learn

  • How do they know about appointments? Do they use Q Flow?
  • Under what circumstances would they contact a claimant?
  • What do they use LMS for after the interview has ended?
  • Is the file uploader intuitive?
  • Do work coaches need access to the information users have submitted in the digital claim form?
  • Do they understand which status to select and when?
  • Do they know what to do after verifying a claim? If so, what do they do?
  • Do they understand that they don’t need to upload the claimant commitment and identity documents?
  • Do they enter information such as their staff number when providing a reason for disallowing a claim?
  • Do they need to be able to disallow a claim or is it enough to either leave the status or mark it as ‘new appointment needed’
  • Can they add a clerical claim?
  • When adding a clerical form, do they upload the claim form and supporting evidence separately?
  • Do they understand what ‘view or update the claim’ means?
  • Is the information in the blue header useful?
  • They spot the ‘special rules’ message
  • Do the email templates work as expected on the machines work coaches use?
  • Are the email templates intuitive?
  • Do they realise they can search by name and National Insurance number?
  • Do they choose to search by name or National Insurance number?

What we learned

Current process for clerical claims

  • Work coaches look through the claim form before seeing the claimant.
  • Processes vary from work coach to work coach:
    • What is the correct process for booking another appointment (if haven’t got enough ID etc)?
    • When should a bio test be done? Why can’t it be done if a claimant has forgotten their ID?
    • We need to make it super clear that ID and claimant commitment don’t need to be uploaded
  • Can we eliminate the need for follow up ID appointments by always doing a bio check?
  • Half of claimants who’ve completed a clerical form need to make a change when they see a work coach before it gets sent to be processed. A clear need here.
  • The only relevant things on the claimant commitment for ESA claimants are the information about letting us know about change of circumstances and to continue sending fit notes.
  • Work coaches need to enter information in LMS that isn’t used, just because LMS requires it. LMS is only really used for a conversation history. Work coaches like the UC system because the conversation history is in one place and clear.

General UI

  • Special rules notice didn’t seem to be spotted as clearly – might need to do some more testing on styles of this.
  • Didn’t seem to be any important info missing from blue header. Most seem to find it all useful in order to quickly check that they’re looking at the right person, rather than having to go into the full claim form to check.
  • ‘View or update the claim’ was understood.

Searching for a claimant

  • Work coaches are used to typing lowercase National Insurance numbers in the search box.
  • Being able to search by name is useful, especially if there are no results for a National Insurance number.
  • Work coaches are used to searching for a claimant using their name. They often abbreviate the name when typing, so they could enter ‘joh smi’ instead of typing ‘john smith’.

Statuses

  • The ‘Verified’ status was understood.
  • ‘New appointment needed’ doesn’t seem like quite the right label. Seems like this should be split up into two statuses - ‘failed to attend’ and ‘follow up appointment needed’ perhaps?
  • ‘Fail to attend’ is term that is understood.
  • What about a status if they book the new appointment themselves? More to think about here…
  • Maybe don’t need the notes text box if we have an explicit ‘failed to attend’ status.
  • The ‘withdrawn (or duplicate)’ status was understood.
  • All work coaches entered information in the text box that appeared. None felt the need to enter any information about who they were (staff number etc).
  • One work coach questioned whether the supporting evidence related to this claim or was generic – this could be made clearer.
  • There was no need identified for work coaches to be able to ‘disallow’ a claim.

Duplicate claims

  • Work coaches understood what they’d need to do if someone had come in and completed two forms – withdraw the one that wasn’t need.

File uploads

  • File upload process caused no issues. All work coaches had used this pattern in the UC system.
  • Work coaches will need a way of adding attachments after a claim has been verified - would be ideal for them to let processors know that a claimant will be giving evidence to them, rather than processors chasing up the same stuff.
  • One work coach said a notes box to say which documents are being uploaded would be useful. This is worth exploring and is something we do currently with in email template..

Adding a new clerical claim

  • No issues adding a clerical claim.

Email templates

  • Email templates worked fine on the work coach we tested them with instinctively knew how to populate them with the correct information. The hyphens weren’t displaying correctly, so we should change how these are being encoded.
