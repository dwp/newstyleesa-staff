Work coach view – version 2

Changes in this version

General

  • The prototype kit was updated to include new styles (colours and focus styles)

Claimant screen

  • List of identity documents and how they’re displayed
  • Added the claimant’s name when referring to the supporting evidence to show that this is specifically aimed at this claimant
  • Added a link to ‘view claim history’ (new screen)

Change the status of this claim

  • Replaced the ‘New appointment needed’ status with ‘Failed to attend’ and ‘Could not be verified’
  • The ‘Failed to attend’ status has an optional text box asking for more detail

Verifying a claim

  • Swapped hint text for clearer ‘Do not include their claimant commitment or identity documents.’
  • Added an additional step with the ability to check uploaded evidence and add notes
  • If no evidence has been uploaded, notes can be added to give the work coach an opportunity to explain more

Could not be verified

  • We ask why the claim couldn’t be verified – this is so that we know if it eventually needs to be disallowed (the appropriate letter can be sent etc)
  • If the claimant wants another appointment, the work coach can either book it for them or pass it onto someone else to book

Search results screen

  • Added National Insurance number to search results listing

Add a new clerical claim

  • Added step to add notes on the uploaded documents
  • Check your answers comes at the end (this allows work coaches to review attachments and notes)

What we want to learn

  • Is the information in the blue header useful?
  • Do they spot the ‘special rules’ message
  • Do they what ‘view claim history’ means?
  • Do they understand the claim history timeline?
  • What do they type in the ‘notes’ textboxes?
  • Do they understand what all the statuses mean?
  • Do they read ‘Do not include their claimant commitment or identity documents’?
  • Do they know to verify the claim if the claimant hasn’t brought any evidence with them?
  • If a claimant is bringing in further evidence, do they know how to add this?
  • Are they able to deal with a failed to attend case?
  • Does the work coach have any more questions after marking a claim as ‘failed to attend’?
  • Do they understand what to do when they find that a claimant previously failed to attend their appointment?
  • Do they understand when to use the ‘Could not be verified status’?
  • Does the new identity content make sense?
  • Do they understand that supporting evidence is specific to that claimant?
  • Do they understand the term ‘biometric check’?
  • Can they add a clerical claim?
  • Do they know how to check documents they’ve uploaded?

Scenarios to test

  1. Barney Rubble, QQ111111Z

    1. Scenario 1, Part A

      Barney turns up, has ID and most of evidence. He doesn’t have his pension statement. He says he’ll bring his pension statement in tomorrow.

      Changes status to ‘Verified’, uploads supporting evidence and may add a note

    2. Scenario 1, Part B

      Tomorrow comes and Barney brings in his pension statement.

      Uploads additional evidence

  2. Buzz Lightyear, QQ000011A

    Scenario 2

    Buzz hasn’t arrived for his appointment.

    Changes status to ‘Failed to attend’ and may add a note

  3. Micky Mouse, QQ112233Z

    1. Scenario 3, Part A

      Micky arrives and provides evidence. He forgets his ID.

      Changes status to ‘Could not be verified’, follows process for follow-up appointment

    2. Scenario 3, Part B

      Minnie comes back in with ID

      Changes status to ‘Verified’, uploads supporting evidence

  4. Minnie Mouse, QQ010101A

    1. Scenario 4. Part A

      Minnie proves ID, but doesn’t have any evidence

      Chooses ‘Could not be verified’ and follows process to book another appointment

    2. Scenario 4. Part B

      Minnie comes back in with evidence

      View the ‘Verified’ claim and uploads supporting evidence

  5. Marge Simpson, QQ231234Z

    1. Scenario 5. Part A

      Marge decides not to sign the claimant commitment and doesn’t want a follow-up appointment.

      Agent changes status to ‘Could not be verified’, select reason and may add a note, selects no appointment needed

    2. Scenario 5. Part B

      Marge changes her mind and contacts the jobcentre.

      Once claimant commitment is complete is complete, agent changes status to ‘New appointment needed’

  6. Homer Simpson, QQ112233C

    Scenario 6, Part A

    Homer has filled in multiple application forms – they realised they needed to change some information, so they filled the form in again.

    Checks which form to use with claimant, then changes status to ‘Verified’, uploads supporting evidence, may add a note and changes status to ‘Withdrawn’ for all other application forms

  7. Bugs Bunny, QQ123456Z

    Scenario 7

    Bugs thought he filled completed the digital form, but it can’t be found. He ends up completing a paper claim form.

    Agent searches for claimant to begin with and then follows the process to add a clerical claim

  8. Fred Flintstone, QQ112233B

    Scenario 8

    Fred Flintstone has contacted the jobcentre to enquire about their claim as they haven't received any money yet. They failed to attend their last appointment.

    Books another appointment for the claimant

  9. Clark Kent, QQ112233A

    Scenario 9

    Clark has contacted the Jobcentre to enquire about their claim as they haven't received any money yet. Their claim was previously withdrawn.

    Asks claimant to make a new application
